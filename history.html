History

Edgar Allan Poe establishes Nevermore Academy

1791

A progenitor of horror and science fiction, Edgar Allan Poe, lies a foundation for Nevermore College.An avid advocate of the literary craft and magic, the famous American poet believed educating young minds should occur in the undisturbed environmnent, opting for a mysterious chateau belonging to Lady Addams in the forest-enclosed Nevermore Hills. The first unofficial group of 12 students gathered in November 1791 to study Practical Witchcraft course.

The commencement of tertiary studies in Magic & Sorcery

1883

As the first college in the history of Nevermore, the school opened its first official program in Magic & Sorcery in the midst of the Industrial Revolution. Initially frowned-up, the programme enjoyed a intense growth in popularity with numerous alumni trained as Professional Witches,Sorcerers and Clairvoyant Physicians.

The first college in Nevermore to accept female students

1887

In the midst of the suffrage revolution, Nevermore Academy took a vote to accept female studies into its halls. Declared "an ancient backbone of all magical and occult", women were offered the same opportunities to study as their male counterparts, and were allowed to take examinations in order to prove their intellectual capacity.

Liberal Arts & Teaching Programs open in the academy

1974

With well-established programs in Magic & Sorcery, Nevermore Academy paid tribute to its literary founder, expanding its offer to Liberal Arts & Communication and Education & Teaching programmes.

Nevermore Academy changes into Nevermore College

2013

After a successful accreditation of all its programs and an agreement with The Miscatonic University, Nevermore Academy transitioned into Nevermore College.Owing to the dedicaion of its brilliant staff, teaching body and talented students, Nevemore College continues to develop and offer programmes based on ancient knowledge and experience.
